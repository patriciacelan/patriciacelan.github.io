Activism
It's time to make a difference.

Families are separated.
Millions are concentrated in camps, waiting for death.
Scientific and cosmetic experiments are done on unwilling participants.
Gas chambers are used to kill billions per year.

What could this be referring to?
What makes someone entitled to be offended by the comparison?
Why are the people who have the most right to be offended… agreeing with the comparison?

Historically, there has always been an “inferior” group, which “superior” people felt entitled to abuse.

Not that long ago, the consent of an “inferior” person did not matter when hurting that person or when using that person like an object or commodity. The “superior” group could do as they pleased to Jews, women, children, elders, blacks, homosexuals, immigrants, and many other historically persecuted groups.
So what gives some people supremacy over other people?

Today, the progressive part of society has concluded:
Abusing any “inferior” groups is unacceptable.
They were never inferior. Consent is essential.
Are you in the progressive part of society that agrees with that?

Yet a different “inferior” group of living beings is being commodified and abused.
What do you, as a progressive member of society, think about this?

Every year, trillions of animals are subjected to intense oppression, abuse, and exploitation. Separated from their families, concentrated in factory farms, forced to undergo painful experiments, and killed by gas chambers, guns, blades, and more.
The current prevailing perspective is that animals are inferior to humans.
“They exist for our use.”

But these animals did not consent to be experimented on, eaten, or killed. One day, humanity will look back on this period of mass exploitation of animals with horror and shame.
How could we have been so heartless in the past? We may not speak the same language as these animals, but don’t they also wish to live in peace, the way anyone does?
What gives humans supremacy over another living species?

"Okay sure, that’s terrible. But opting out of supporting animal exploitation would be a huge change from the way I have lived my whole life. It’s too difficult to become vegan.”

That's what I used to think. But you might be surprised...
As a vegan since 2014, I am able to eat almost the exact same diet I used to eat.
There are plenty of animal product alternatives which taste similar to the products they mimic. I used to love meat, eggs, dairy, and various snacks and junk foods that contain animal products. I don’t miss any of them one bit. My quality of life is the same.
I eat what I want – with a clear conscience.
