Psychiatry
Areas of Practice

Psychiatry is an exciting yet complicated field of medicine.
There are many challenges in the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of mental illness.
For example, due to overlap in diagnostic criteria for several disorders, due to some psychiatrists preferring older versions of the DSM, and due to some psychiatrists working from a place of stereotypes and confirmation bias, many patients can struggle with being misdiagnosed and inadequately treated for years before they find the care they need to heal.

My goal is to support my patients with a trauma-informed, neurodiversity-affirming approach, provide care as an evidence-based practitioner, and keep up-to-date on research developments.

I aim to be open-minded and work collaboratively with my patients in exploring diagnostic possibilities and creating a treatment approach that will meet the patient's goals for physical, psychological, and social wellbeing. My primary approach to treating mental illness is utilizing psychotherapy while optimizing physical health, with psychiatric medication as a possible adjunct only when necessary for supporting progress.

As an MD currently in psychiatry training, I cannot take on patients independently yet.
However, I aspire to open a psychotherapy clinic in upcoming years, where my focus will be treating trauma disorders, anxiety disorders, mood disorders, and vystopia in both neurotypical and neurodivergent populations, supporting both medical trainees and the general public. This clinic will integrate pet therapy with standardized, structured psychotherapy treatment.
